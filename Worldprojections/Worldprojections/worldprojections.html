In this project I learned how to display images in different projections

Describe in your own words how you displayed the map in different projections using QGIS

I projected a vector shape file of the countries of the world. Then, while running the indicatrix mapper plugin, I searched for different projections by using the filter word "world". I tried a bunch of different ones because some of them didn't seem to work. I finally found the ESRI 54052 and the EPSG 4087 projections which worked well.

ESPG 3857 Projection

This projection displays the world to be flat. To accomodate this, the latitudes closer to the poles have larger areas.

ESRI 53018 Projection

This projection tries to still maintain the spherical shape of earth. The relative shapes of the continents aren't changed much. The closer to the center of the map the circles are, the less distored they become.

ESRI 54034 Projection

On this display, it is in equal area projection so there is distortion of the continents the farther from the center you go.

ESRI 54027 Projection

This is a conic projection. You can see this in how the grid does not wrap all around as if a cone was unrolled. The closer to the north pole places are on the map, the less distorted they get.

ESRI 102016 Projection

The azimuthal projection looks similar to the conic projection. However, the ends of the grid are closer together, and as a result the shapes of the continents are distorted to a higher degree than seen in the conic projection.

EPSG 4087 Projection

This is very similar to the WGS 84 example at the top of the page. Perhaps the area is distributed slightly different between the two.

ESRI 54052 Projection

The circles vary in how they are distorted. It is interesting that at latitudes near the poles we see the cirlces getting closer together.
